**MedDefense Strategic Board Briefing**

Members of the Board,

Our current security posture is unsustainably fragile. While our outer digital perimeter effectively blocks standard external threats, our internal hospital network is completely undefended, leaving critical patient care databases entirely exposed. 

Our most dangerous gap is a completely flat network layout. High-risk public guest Wi-Fi, employee laptops, unpatched medical devices, and our primary electronic medical records database all share the exact same digital workspace. If a threat actor compromises a single public waiting room terminal via standard email deception, they gain immediate, unhindered access to our entire patient directory. A single infection can spread laterally to paralyze hospital operations and encrypt live patient databases alongside our local data backups simultaneously.

To prevent an operational shutdown, we must execute three immediate, cost-optimized actions:

First, **Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication** across all remote network connections and medical record login screens within 30 days to neutralize stolen password abuse. *Cost: $11,500.*

Second, **Establish Cloud Backup Isolation** within 30 days by copying nightly data points to an off-site, unalterable cloud vault. This guarantees system recovery even during a full ransomware incident. *Cost: $9,000.*

Third, **Isolate the Internal Network** into distinct, firewalled compartments within 60 days. This micro-segmentation walls off high-value clinical servers from general workstations and medical devices, stopping lateral threat movement. *Cost: $0 (utilizes existing hardware and internal engineering hours).*

Approving our $120,000 security budget is not an administrative expense; it is a vital investment in clinical continuity. For an immediate outlay of $93,000, we insulate MedDefense against catastrophic operational paralysis, regulatory penalties, and millions in healthcare litigation. 

If the Board chooses non-action today, we accept the near-certainty of an unrecoverable cyber attack that halts patient care, compromises life-safety operations, and permanently destroys public trust.
