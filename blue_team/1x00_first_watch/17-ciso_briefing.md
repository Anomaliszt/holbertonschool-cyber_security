# Strategic Board Briefing: Clinical Continuity & Risk Mitigation

Our current digital defenses are unsustainably fragile. While our outer security wall effectively blocks basic internet threats, our internal network is completely unpartitioned. Once an intruder slips past the outside wall, they encounter zero internal boundaries, leaving our most sensitive patient databases completely exposed.

Our single greatest failure point is that our network operates like an open, single-room warehouse. High-risk public waiting room Wi-Fi, office laptops, unpatched medical equipment, and our primary patient database all share the exact same digital space. If a criminal compromises a single reception computer through a deceptive email link, they gain instant, unrestricted access to everything. A lone infection can freely spread across the facility, simultaneously paralyzing patient care applications and destroying our local emergency backups.

To guarantee continuous hospital operations, we must authorize three immediate actions:

1. **Lock the Front Doors (Next 30 Days):** Enforce strict identity verification checks for all remote logins and medical records access points to neutralize stolen passwords. *Cost: $11,500.*
2. **Secure a Safe House (Next 30 Days):** Copy our vital data every night to an isolated, unalterable off-site cloud vault, ensuring we can recover our systems even during a worst-case cyber attack. *Cost: $9,000.*
3. **Build Firewalls (Next 60 Days):** Separate our internal network into isolated digital compartments. This walls off critical clinical servers from general staff computers and medical devices, trapping any potential infection in its tracks. *Cost: $0 (utilizes existing equipment and internal staff).*

Approving this $120,000 security budget is a direct investment in patient safety and clinical continuity, protecting us against millions in regulatory penalties and lawsuits. If we take no action today, we accept the near-certainty of an unrecoverable attack that shuts down care, threatens lives, and permanently breaks public trust.
