## 1. Data Lifecycle and Protection Registry

The table below traces the lifecycle states of MedDefense's data assets to map where security controls fail to meet compliance and risk thresholds.

| **Patient Medical Records (EHR Data)** | **Restricted** | `ehr-db-01` (PostgreSQL database); Nightly backup sets on `NAS-01`. | Routed over internal LAN to workstations; Remote access via site-to-site VPN. | Displayed to clinical staff (nurses, physicians) on floor workstations and iPads. | Database server firewall rules; Hardened SSH on `ehr-srv-01`; Basic password policy. | **In Use:** Workstations left logged in/idle for 15+ minutes at nurse stations. <br>**At Rest:** Backups on `NAS-01` are unencrypted and colocated in the same server room rack row. |

## 2. Strategic Data Risk Summary

MedDefense's most critical data protection weakness lies in its **Data-in-Use and Data-at-Rest vulnerabilities within its Restricted Patient Medical Records (EHR) lifecycle.** Despite holding a "Restricted" classification level—where a compromise directly threatens patient safety and guarantees severe regulatory penalties—the protections are deeply flawed. 



Data-in-use is routinely exposed to public foot traffic via unattended nurse workstations left authenticated to the EHR for over 15 minutes due to a culture that discourages logging out. Simultaneously, data-at-rest is stored in unencrypted, unsegmented nightly backup volumes sitting on a local NAS in the exact same rack row as the production environment. This gap leaves MedDefense highly exposed; an attacker bypassing perimeter defenses can silently read patient records off unencrypted backup disks or alter clinical charts on abandoned terminals, directly impacting patient care and violating HIPAA mandates.
