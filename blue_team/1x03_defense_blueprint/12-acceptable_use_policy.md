# MedDefense Health Systems Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

**Effective Date:** [To be approved]  
**Owner:** Deputy CISO  
**Applies To:** All employees, physicians, contractors, volunteers, students, third parties, and temporary staff using MedDefense systems or data.

## 1. Purpose and Scope
This policy defines how MedDefense information systems, network resources, email, mobile devices, removable media, and data may be used. It applies to all MedDefense-owned systems, all systems connected to the MedDefense network, and all workforce members who access MedDefense data, including Protected Health Information (PHI), financial records, and credentials.

## 2. Acceptable Use of Systems
Users may use MedDefense systems only for approved business, clinical, educational, and operational purposes. Limited personal use is permitted only when it does not interfere with patient care, violate law or policy, consume excessive resources, or introduce security risk. Users must access only the systems and data required for their role and must follow department procedures for handling patient, financial, and administrative information.

## 3. Prohibited Activities
The following activities are prohibited:
- Sharing passwords, MFA prompts, badges, or generic logins.
- Installing unapproved software, browser extensions, remote-access tools, or peer-to-peer software.
- Attempting to bypass security controls, firewalls, segmentation rules, endpoint protection, or logging.
- Accessing patient records without a legitimate job-related need.
- Exporting large volumes of PHI, billing data, or user lists without management approval.
- Connecting unauthorized servers, routers, Wi-Fi devices, Raspberry Pi systems, or personal laptops to the MedDefense network.
- Using MedDefense systems to store, transmit, or process unlawful, offensive, or non-business material.

## 4. Personal Devices and Removable Media
Personal laptops may not connect to internal MedDefense wired networks. Personal phones and tablets may access approved guest or managed mobile services only. USB storage devices are prohibited unless explicitly approved by IT and encrypted; exceptions must be documented for clinical or biomedical workflow needs. This rule directly addresses prior findings involving shadow IT, unmanaged devices, and removable media exposure.

## 5. Password and Authentication Requirements
All users must maintain unique passwords and may not reuse MedDefense passwords on external services. MFA is required for VPN, administrative accounts, and any system designated by IT or Security as high risk. Users must never approve an MFA prompt they did not initiate and must report suspicious login prompts immediately. Shared accounts are prohibited unless formally approved, documented, and monitored for a specific operational need.

## 6. Data Handling Requirements
- **Restricted data** (PHI, credentials, security configurations): access only from authorized systems; do not copy to personal devices, personal email, or unsanctioned cloud storage.
- **Confidential data** (financial, HR, internal operations): share only with authorized workforce members and approved third parties.
- Printed PHI or financial documents must be collected promptly and secured when not in use.
- Email containing sensitive data must use approved secure methods.
- Users must follow retention and disposal requirements for paper and electronic records.

## 7. Monitoring and Enforcement
MedDefense monitors network activity, login activity, endpoint events, email usage, VPN sessions, and access to sensitive records to protect patients, staff, and systems. Users should have no expectation of privacy when using MedDefense systems, except as otherwise required by law. Suspected violations may result in access restriction, investigation, disciplinary action up to termination, contractor offboarding, legal action, and regulatory reporting where required.

## 8. Reporting
Users must immediately report phishing attempts, lost devices, accidental disclosure, suspicious USB use, unusual MFA prompts, or unauthorized access concerns to IT/Security. Prompt reporting will be treated as a positive security action; failure to report may be treated as a policy violation.

## 9. Exceptions
Any exception to this policy must be approved in writing by the Deputy CISO and the relevant business owner. Exceptions involving PHI, medical devices, or legacy systems must include a compensating control and an expiration date.
